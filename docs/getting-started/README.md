# Getting Started with WP Sell Services

Turn your WordPress website into a thriving marketplace where talented people sell their services and customers find exactly the help they need.

## What Can You Build?

Imagine having your own Fiverr or Upwork - but on your website, under your brand. That's what WP Sell Services gives you.

**Here are some ideas:**

- A design marketplace where graphic artists sell logos, banners, and illustrations
- A writing platform where content creators offer blog posts, copywriting, and editing
- A tech services hub where developers build websites, fix bugs, and create apps
- A consulting directory where experts sell their knowledge and time
- A creative community where musicians, voice actors, and video editors find work

You earn money from every sale without doing the work yourself. Your job is to bring buyers and sellers together.

## How It Works

The whole process is straightforward:

1. **Someone creates a service** - "I'll design your business logo for $75"
2. **A buyer finds it and orders** - They pay through your website
3. **The seller delivers the work** - The buyer gets their logo
4. **Everyone wins** - The seller gets paid, the buyer gets their work, and you earn a commission

That's it. The plugin handles all the details - payments, messaging, file delivery, reviews, and payouts.

## Setting Up (About 10 Minutes)

### Install the Plugin

1. In your WordPress dashboard, go to **Plugins → Add New**
2. Click **Upload Plugin** and select the wp-sell-services.zip file
3. Click **Install Now**, then **Activate**

Done! The plugin creates everything it needs automatically.

### Set Your Commission

This is how you make money. Go to **WP Sell Services → Settings → Commission**.

The default is 20%, which means:
- When someone sells a $100 service, you keep $20
- They receive $80 for their work

Most marketplaces charge between 10% and 30%. Start wherever feels fair and adjust based on what works.

### Choose How Payments Work

Go to **WP Sell Services → Settings → General**.

For most websites, choose **WooCommerce**. It's free, well-supported, and works with PayPal, Stripe, credit cards, and dozens of other payment options.

If you already have WooCommerce set up with payment gateways, you're ready to go!

### Check Your Pages

The plugin creates these pages for you:

| Page | What It Does |
|------|--------------|
| Services | Where people browse and find services |
| Become a Vendor | Where sellers sign up to offer their services |
| Dashboard | Where users manage their orders and messages |

Visit each page to make sure they're working. You can customize them like any WordPress page.

## Creating Your First Service

Want to test how everything works? Create a sample service yourself, or invite someone to sign up.

**To create a service:**

1. Go to the "Become a Vendor" page and register (or create a vendor account through the admin)
2. Visit the Dashboard and click **Create Service**
3. Fill in the details:
   - Give it a clear title like "Professional Logo Design"
   - Write a description of what buyers will get
   - Set up pricing packages (you can have a basic, standard, and premium option)
   - Add images showing examples of your work
   - Choose how long delivery takes
4. Submit and wait for approval (if you've enabled moderation) or it goes live immediately

## What Buyers Experience

When someone visits your marketplace:

1. They browse services or search for what they need
2. They click on something that looks good
3. They pick a package and click **Order Now**
4. They pay through your checkout
5. They tell the seller what they need (project requirements)
6. The seller creates the work and delivers it
7. The buyer reviews the delivery and can request changes if needed
8. Once happy, they accept the delivery and can leave a review

The whole process happens on your website. You're notified of important events, but buyers and sellers communicate directly.

## What Sellers Experience

People who sign up to sell services:

1. Register through the "Become a Vendor" page
2. Create service listings with prices, packages, and examples
3. Receive orders in their dashboard
4. Chat with buyers about project details
5. Deliver their work through the platform
6. Get paid after a short clearance period (usually 7-14 days)
7. Build up reviews and reputation over time

## Common Questions

**Do I need any other plugins?**
Just WooCommerce for handling payments. It's free and takes about 5 minutes to set up with PayPal or Stripe.

**How do sellers get their money?**
They request payouts from their dashboard. You review and approve them, then send payment through PayPal, bank transfer, or however you prefer.

**Can I approve services before they go live?**
Yes! Turn on service moderation in your settings, and you'll review every listing before it appears on your site.

**What if there's a problem with an order?**
Buyers and sellers can open disputes, and you step in to help resolve them. The plugin has a built-in dispute system.

**Can I customize how everything looks?**
The plugin works with any WordPress theme. For deeper customization, templates can be overridden in your theme folder.
