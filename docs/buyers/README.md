# Finding and Buying Services

This guide helps you find the right person for your project and get great results.

## Finding What You Need

### Browse by Category

The services page groups everything into categories like:
- Web Development
- Graphic Design
- Writing & Content
- Marketing
- Video & Animation

Click any category to see what's available.

### Search for Something Specific

Use the search bar to find exactly what you're looking for:

**Good searches:**
- "WordPress website" - finds people who build WordPress sites
- "logo design" - finds designers who create logos
- "SEO audit" - finds marketing experts who check your website

**Too vague:**
- "help" - could mean anything
- "cheap" - prices vary a lot
- "best" - everyone thinks they're the best

### Narrow Down Your Results

Use filters to find the perfect match:
- **Price range** - Stay within your budget
- **Rating** - Only see highly-rated sellers
- **Delivery time** - Find people who can work quickly

### Choosing the Right Person

Before you order, check:

| Look For | Why It Matters |
|----------|---------------|
| High ratings (4+ stars) | Other buyers were happy |
| Recent reviews | They're actively working |
| Portfolio examples | You can see their actual work |
| Clear description | They explain exactly what you'll get |
| Quick responses | They communicate well |

## Understanding Prices

### Most Services Have Three Options

Sellers usually offer different packages:

| Package | What You Get |
|---------|-------------|
| Basic | The essentials at the lowest price |
| Standard | The most popular choice with good value |
| Premium | Everything included, fastest delivery |

### What to Compare

Look at these things when choosing a package:
- **What's included** - How many items, revisions, or features
- **How many revisions** - Times you can ask for changes
- **Delivery time** - How many days until you receive the work
- **Extras** - What costs more

### Add-Ons (Extras)

Some sellers offer optional upgrades:
- **Rush delivery** - Get it faster for an extra fee
- **More items** - Like additional logo concepts
- **Source files** - The original editable files
- **Extended revisions** - More rounds of changes

You can add these during checkout.

## Placing Your Order

### Step 1: Pick What You Want

1. Look at all the package options
2. Choose the one that fits your needs
3. Add any extras you want
4. Click **Order Now**

### Step 2: Pay

Complete payment through the checkout. Your money is held safely until you approve the final work.

### Step 3: Tell Them What You Need

After paying, you'll fill out a project brief. This is where you explain exactly what you're looking for.

**Be specific! The more detail you give, the better your results.**

Here's an example for a website header design:

```
Project: Header Design for My Website

About my business: Acme Software - we make business tools
Industry: B2B Software

What I need:
- Size: 1920 x 400 pixels
- Include my logo (I've attached it below)
- Use my brand colors: blue (#2563EB) and dark gray (#1E293B)
- Add our tagline: "Software that works"
- Modern, professional look

Style I like: Check out stripe.com - I love how clean it looks

Attached files:
- logo.svg
- brand-guidelines.pdf
```

The more you share upfront, the fewer back-and-forth messages you'll need.

## Following Your Order

### What Each Status Means

| Status | What's Happening |
|--------|-----------------|
| Pending Payment | Complete your checkout |
| Pending Requirements | Fill in your project details |
| In Progress | The seller is working on it |
| Delivered | They've submitted work for you to review |
| Revision Requested | You've asked for changes |
| Completed | You've approved the final work |
| Cancelled | The order was cancelled |
| Disputed | There's an issue being resolved |

### Messaging Your Seller

Use the order conversation to:
- Ask questions about your project
- Provide additional information
- Request progress updates
- Discuss changes

**Tips for good communication:**
- Be clear about what you want
- Reply quickly when they ask questions
- Keep it professional
- Use the platform's messaging (not external email)

## Getting Your Work

### When Work Is Delivered

The seller will submit their completed work. Here's what to do:

1. Download everything they've sent
2. Check it against what you asked for
3. If it's development work, test that it actually works
4. Make sure you got everything promised

### Your Options

After reviewing the delivery:

| Choice | When to Use It |
|--------|---------------|
| **Accept** | You're happy with the work |
| **Request Changes** | Small fixes needed |
| **Open Dispute** | Serious problems, seller not responsive |

### Asking for Changes

Revisions are normal - that's why packages include them. When asking for changes:

1. Point out exactly what needs fixing
2. Reference your original requirements
3. Give examples if helpful
4. Be fair and reasonable

**A good revision request:**
```
Thanks for the draft! Two small changes please:

1. The headline font doesn't match my brand guidelines.
   Can you use "Inter" font as shown in my PDF?

2. The blue looks too light on my screen.
   Let's try a darker shade.

Everything else looks great!
```

**Avoid:**
- Asking for things outside the original scope
- Vague feedback like "make it better"
- Endless revision requests beyond your package limit

## Leaving Reviews

### After Your Order Completes

You can rate your experience:
- **Overall rating** - 1 to 5 stars
- **Communication** - How responsive were they?
- **Quality** - Did the work meet expectations?
- **Value** - Was it worth the price?
- **Written feedback** - Share your experience

### Writing Helpful Reviews

**A helpful review:**
```
John delivered exactly what I asked for. The logo looks professional
and works great across all my materials. He responded to messages
within a few hours and incorporated my feedback perfectly. Would
definitely order again.
```

**Less helpful:**
```
Good.
```

Your honest review helps other buyers and rewards sellers who do great work.

## Posting a Custom Request

### Let Sellers Come to You

Instead of browsing, describe what you need and let sellers pitch you.

### Creating a Request

1. Go to the **Buyer Requests** page
2. Click **Post a Request**
3. Describe your project:
   - Title: What you need in a few words
   - Description: Full details of the project
   - Budget: How much you can spend
   - Deadline: When you need it done

### Example Request

```
Title: Need WordPress Plugin Customized

What I need:
I have a contact form plugin that needs some modifications.

Current situation:
- Using Contact Form 7
- Works fine, but need extra features

Requirements:
1. Add show/hide fields based on answers
2. Connect the form to our CRM system
3. Add file upload with size limits

Budget: $200-400
Deadline: 2 weeks

Please share examples of similar work you've done.
```

### Reviewing Offers

Sellers will send proposals with:
- Their price
- Delivery timeline
- Message explaining their approach

Pick the one who understands your project, has relevant experience, and offers fair pricing.

## Contacting Sellers Before Ordering

### Have Questions First?

You might want to ask something before placing an order.

**To message a seller:**
1. Visit their service page
2. Click **Contact Seller** or **Ask a Question**
3. Write your message
4. Check your Messages for their reply

**Good pre-order questions:**
- "Can you handle [specific requirement]?"
- "Are you available to start this week?"
- "Do you have experience with [specific tool/technology]?"
- "Can you show me examples of similar work?"

## Handling Problems

### When to Ask for Changes

Use revisions when:
- Small tweaks are needed
- Something doesn't match what you asked for
- The seller missed a detail

### When to Open a Dispute

Open a dispute when:
- The seller isn't responding
- The work is completely different from what was promised
- Files are corrupted or won't open
- The seller refuses reasonable changes

### How Disputes Work

1. Click **Open Dispute** on your order
2. Explain the problem clearly
3. Attach screenshots or files as evidence
4. Wait for an admin to review
5. Follow their guidance

Most disputes end with:
- The seller making corrections
- A partial refund
- A full refund and cancellation

## Common Questions

**Can I cancel my order?**
You can request a cancellation, but the seller needs to agree. It's easier to cancel before work begins.

**What if the seller is taking too long?**
Message them first. If they don't respond for a while, contact support or open a dispute.

**Can I order from multiple sellers at once?**
Yes! Each order is separate. Manage them from your dashboard.

**Is my payment safe?**
Your money is held by the platform until you accept the delivery. If something goes wrong, you're protected.

**What if I need ongoing work?**
Many sellers offer repeat orders or packages for ongoing projects. Message them to discuss.
