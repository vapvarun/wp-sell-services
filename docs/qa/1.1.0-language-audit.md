# 1.1.0 Language Audit — Findings

Audit target: vendor video intro, NET earnings surfaces, tips regression, extensions (catalog orders), milestones (ad-hoc + Upwork-style), and 7 sub-order email templates shipped 2026-04-21.

## Summary
- Total findings: 27
- Critical (broken / misleading): 3
- High (confusing): 9
- Medium (inconsistent): 11
- Low (nice-to-have polish): 4

## Critical findings

### C1 — "Milestone cancelled" shown to buyer for a decline they just performed
**File:** `templates/order/milestone-view.php:82-95`
**Current:** For `cancelled` status the vendor sees "Milestone cancelled" and the buyer also sees "Milestone cancelled". The same `cancelled` status is set both when the vendor cancels their proposal and when the buyer declines it (`wpss_decline_milestone` vs `wpss_delete_milestone` converge on the same sub-order status).
**Issue:** A buyer who just clicked "Decline" is told the milestone is "cancelled" with no indication of who did it or that the seller may propose a revised phase. It reads like the seller cancelled on them. The parallel flow on the order-view list row (`order-view.php:1254`) uses the same ambiguous "Cancelled" label. The JS confirm ("Decline this milestone? The seller can propose a revised one.") promises a next step the UI then hides.
**Suggested:** Differentiate the copy by actor. Buyer-declined: "Declined — your seller can send a revised phase." Vendor-cancelled: "Proposal cancelled by seller." Easiest path: persist the actor in meta at decline/delete time and branch on it in the template.

### C2 — "Paid · seller working" shown to buyer after *they* declined
**File:** `templates/order/order-view.php:1244`
**Current:** The in_progress state label is `$is_vendor ? 'Paid · ready for delivery' : 'Paid · seller working'`. Correct as written, but there is no `case 'cancelled'` branch rendered for the buyer in list context (`order-view.php:1252-1254`) — the cancelled label just says "Cancelled" with no context of who cancelled or what happens next.
**Issue:** Combined with C1 above, the buyer-side timeline shows a terminal "Cancelled" row with no cue that the seller can repropose. Buyers will read this as "the phase is dead" and message the seller to ask what happened — avoidable support volume.
**Suggested:** Mirror the C1 fix — render "Declined by you" or "Cancelled by seller" in the list state label as well, not just on the detail view.

### C3 — Order-view extension pending card (buyer side) mis-credits the seller
**File:** `templates/order/order-view.php:1517`
**Current:** "Your seller sent a quote for the extra work you asked about."
**Issue:** Extensions can be initiated by the vendor without a prior buyer ask (the vendor CTA copy at line 1594 literally says "Fixed-price order, buyer already paid the base. For small add-ons on top, quote the extra amount…"). Asserting "the extra work you asked about" in the buyer email/card when the buyer did not ask will read as pushy or dishonest. Same wording is reused in `NotificationService.php:1102`.
**Issue severity:** Critical because it puts words in the buyer's mouth — perceived as shady upsell pressure.
**Suggested:** "Your seller sent a quote for some extra work on this order. Pay to approve — they will continue on the expanded scope. If the quote is off, decline and discuss in chat first."

## High findings

### H1 — "Milestone-based" radio label vs "Phase" / "Milestone" language elsewhere
**File:** `templates/single-request.php:530`
**Current:** Radio option reads `**Milestone-based** — phased plan, buyer pays each phase as you deliver.`
**Issue:** The product picks "phase" as the friendly noun in the modal ("Phase title", "+ Add another phase", "Project total") but the radio label reintroduces the jargon word "milestone-based". That word does not appear anywhere else on the proposal form, so the vendor has to infer that "milestone-based" = "phases" = what they are about to fill in. Industry convention (Upwork) is fine with "Milestones" as the header but the radio is the teaching moment — pick one and stick.
**Suggested:** "**Phased project** — split the work into paid phases the buyer funds one at a time." Keep "Milestones" for the section heading downstream.

### H2 — Proposal review breakdown badge reads "N phase milestone plan"
**File:** `templates/single-request.php:302-305`
**Current:** `printf( _n( '%d phase milestone plan', '%d phase milestone plan', count, 'wp-sell-services' ), count )`
**Issue:** "3 phase milestone plan" is ungrammatical (missing hyphen) and pairs both terms ("phase" + "milestone") that we are trying to unify. Singular and plural strings are identical, so the translators' comment is also misleading.
**Suggested:** "%d-phase plan" (e.g. "3-phase plan"). Drop the word "milestone" here — the details element right below is already titled "View milestone breakdown" (see H3 for that one).

### H3 — "View milestone breakdown" details summary
**File:** `templates/single-request.php:322`
**Current:** `View milestone breakdown`
**Issue:** Inside the plan the rows are labelled "Phase title", the total says "Project total", and the breakdown summary says "milestone breakdown". Three names for the same thing on one card.
**Suggested:** "View phase breakdown" (or "See the phases" for friendlier tone).

### H4 — Ad-hoc vendor "+ Propose Milestone" button vs Upwork-style "phases" nomenclature
**File:** `templates/order/order-view.php:1213`
**Current:** Button label `+ Propose Milestone`. The modal it opens is titled "Propose a Milestone" (line 1340) but immediately uses "Phase title" as the first field label (line 1353) and "Break the work into a named phase" as intro copy (line 1349).
**Issue:** Vendor learns three nouns in three seconds: button says Milestone, title says Milestone, field says Phase. If the product has decided Phase is the friendly term, the button and title should lead with it.
**Suggested:** Button: "+ Propose a phase". Title: "Propose a phase". Keep the list section heading as "Milestones (N)" — that is the Upwork pattern and appears to be intentional.

### H5 — Proposal modal contract explainer says "phased plan, buyer pays each phase as you deliver"
**File:** `templates/single-request.php:530`
**Current:** "phased plan, buyer pays each phase as you deliver."
**Issue:** Misleading — the lock-step gating in the milestone service means the buyer pays **before** each phase, not after ("buyer pays each phase as you deliver" implies pay-on-delivery). The modal elsewhere (line 573) correctly says "Each phase is a separate payment the buyer makes when ready."
**Suggested:** "phased plan — buyer pays for each phase before you start it, approves it, then funds the next." Or shorter: "phased plan — one phase paid and delivered at a time."

### H6 — "Buyer asked for extra work?" CTA title assumes the buyer asked
**File:** `templates/order/order-view.php:1593`
**Current:** `Buyer asked for extra work?`
**Issue:** Same trap as C3 — this CTA shows on every eligible fixed-price order regardless of whether the buyer raised it. The question mark softens it but the framing still teaches the vendor to only use this when asked, which undersells the feature. Sellers frequently spot scope creep before the buyer articulates it.
**Suggested:** "Need to quote extra work?" or "Scope growing?" — more inclusive of seller-initiated quotes.

### H7 — "Tip pending payment" is buyer-facing jargon
**File:** `templates/order/tip-view.php:51,53`
**Current:** `Tip pending payment` (shown to both vendor and buyer if the tip order hasn't cleared).
**Issue:** For a buyer who just hit "Send tip" and landed on the receipt with an unpaid status, this reads like a system error. They don't think of themselves as "paying" the tip — they think they sent it. This state mostly shows up when payment failed/abandoned.
**Suggested:** Buyer: "Tip not sent yet — payment didn't complete." Vendor: "Tip pending — buyer hasn't paid yet."

### H8 — "Credited to wallet" / "Credited to your balance" / "Credited to your wallet" — three labels for one concept
**Files:**
- `templates/emails/milestone-paid.php:59` — "Credited to your wallet"
- `templates/emails/extension-approved.php:58` — "Credited to wallet"
- `templates/emails/tip-received.php:88` — "Credited to your balance"
**Issue:** Same underlying event (net amount added to the vendor's withdrawable balance), three phrasings, one of them drops the possessive and one swaps "wallet" for "balance". Vendors will wonder if "balance" and "wallet" are the same thing. Related: the analytics header (`analytics.php:179`) says "Net Earnings", the sales stat card (`sales.php:101`) says "Revenue", the wallet stat (`wallet.php:91`) says "Available Balance" — four names downstream of the same money flow.
**Suggested:** Standardise on "Credited to your wallet" in all three emails. In dashboards: pick either "Balance" or "Wallet" and use consistently — see cross-cutting section.

### H9 — "Available Balance" stat in wallet is ambiguous vs "Pending Withdrawal"
**File:** `wp-sell-services-pro/templates/dashboard/sections/wallet.php:91,95`
**Current:** Stat card reads "Available Balance" with the big number being `$balance` (from `WalletManager::get_balance`). Next card is "Pending Withdrawal".
**Issue:** In wallet semantics `get_balance()` typically *excludes* pending withdrawals — but the label doesn't say so. Vendors will add the two numbers together mentally. "Available" is the key word and it's there, but the stat card format (big number first, small label second) hides it.
**Suggested:** Sub-label: "Ready to withdraw" as a 12px helper under the amount, matching how analytics uses "after platform fee" at line 181. Then vendors don't have to infer.

## Medium findings

### M1 — "Gross" column header on milestone/extension/tip view is unexplained
**Files:**
- `templates/order/milestone-view.php:132` — `Gross`
- `templates/order/extension-view.php:122` — `Gross`
- `templates/order/tip-view.php:87` — `Gross tip`
**Issue:** "Gross" is finance jargon. Buyers don't see this (it is vendor-only per the `if` guards) but vendors unfamiliar with the term will pause. The tip view already uses "Gross tip" which is clearer than bare "Gross".
**Suggested:** "Buyer paid" — matches the wording used in the email templates and the order-list tooltip (`sales.php:237` "Buyer paid %s (gross). You earn the net amount after platform fee.") and removes jargon.

### M2 — "Reference" label on sub-order receipts
**Files:**
- `templates/order/milestone-view.php:126`
- `templates/order/extension-view.php:116`
- `templates/order/tip-view.php:81`
**Current:** `Reference` with the order_number beside it.
**Issue:** Reference to what? Bank transfer references, support tickets, and order numbers all use the word "reference". Buyers will search their email for this number and come up empty because it is the sub-order number, not their parent order number.
**Suggested:** "Phase order #" on milestone, "Extension order #" on extension, "Tip order #" on tip. Or simplest: "Order number".

### M3 — Empty-state for vendor with no milestone yet uses "custom project"
**File:** `templates/order/order-view.php:1222`
**Current:** "This is a custom project — break it into paid phases so the buyer can approve each stage as you deliver."
**Issue:** "custom project" and "request order" and "request-mode" are all used inconsistently across the plugin. A vendor on the dashboard doesn't know the term "request order"; they know it as "the buyer's job post I won". "Custom project" is reasonable but adopt it consistently.
**Suggested:** Keep "custom project" here but update the other surfaces (`wallet.php` ledger uses "Order", analytics uses generic "Orders") to mention "custom project" where the row is a request-mode order. Not blocking for 1.1.0 but worth a follow-up.

### M4 — Capitalisation: "Submit Delivery" vs "Submit delivery" vs "Resubmit delivery"
**Files:**
- `templates/order/milestone-view.php:190` — button `Submit Delivery` (Title Case)
- `templates/order/milestone-view.php:190` — button `Resubmit delivery` (sentence case)
- `templates/order/milestone-view.php:216,234` — modal title + submit button `Submit Delivery` (Title Case)
- `templates/order/order-view.php:1314` — list button `Submit delivery` (sentence case)
- `src/Services/EmailService.php:616` — email heading `Milestone delivered` — unrelated
- `templates/emails/milestone-paid.php:71` — body text "use Submit Delivery on this phase" (Title Case, referring to the button by name)
**Issue:** "Submit Delivery" and "Submit delivery" render on the same screen, sometimes within two clicks of each other. Mixed cap is a tell-tale sign of drift.
**Suggested:** Pick sentence case to match the rest of the button library ("Cancel proposal", "Review & approve", "Open the order", "Accept & Pay"). Update all button labels to "Submit delivery" and update the email body reference accordingly.

### M5 — "Review & approve" vs "Review &amp; Approve" vs "Approve delivery"
**Files:**
- `templates/order/order-view.php:1319` — `Review & approve`
- `templates/emails/milestone-submitted.php:51` — `Review &amp; Approve` (Title Case + HTML entity)
- `templates/order/milestone-view.php:197` — `Approve delivery`
**Issue:** Three labels for the same destination (buyer approving a delivery). The HTML entity `&amp;` in the email is correct escaping but shows up inconsistently vs the plain `&` in the PHP template (both will render identically — just flagging for code consistency).
**Suggested:** Standardise on "Review & approve" everywhere. The email CTA button is the odd one out.

### M6 — "Start work on this phase" button in milestone-paid email vs UI "Submit delivery"
**File:** `templates/emails/milestone-paid.php:66`
**Current:** Email CTA: `Start work on this phase`
**Issue:** Tonally good, but the button links to the sub-order page where the only available action is "Submit Delivery". Vendor clicks "Start work" expecting a start button and instead sees a delivery form. Minor but surprising.
**Suggested:** "Open this phase" or "View this phase". Same link, less mismatch between label and destination.

### M7 — Milestone list row "View milestone" button vs "View" button on list rows
**Files:**
- `templates/order/order-view.php:1323` — "View milestone" (appears on every milestone list item, regardless of state)
- `templates/dashboard/sections/sales.php:254` — "View" for sub-orders, "Manage" for regular service orders
**Issue:** On the parent order page, each milestone row has two buttons when vendor (e.g. "Submit delivery" + "View milestone"). "View milestone" after already seeing the milestone's title, description, amount, and status right above it is redundant — the whole row is already a view.
**Suggested:** Drop the "View milestone" button on the order-view list (row already shows everything). Keep the primary action only. Keep the sales.php "View" button — that one has a dedicated destination.

### M8 — "Cancel proposal" (vendor) vs "Decline" (buyer) — consistent enough, flagging for symmetry
**Files:** `templates/order/order-view.php:1305,1309`, `templates/order/milestone-view.php:176,183`
**Current:** Buyer gets "Decline"; vendor gets "Cancel proposal".
**Issue:** Works fine but they converge on the same `cancelled` status, so the audit trail loses who did what (see C1). Not a label problem per se, but once C1 is fixed the labels are sensible.
**Suggested:** No change needed to the labels themselves.

### M9 — Extension list/card uses "Quote" but sub-order badge in dashboard says "Extension"
**Files:**
- `templates/dashboard/sections/sales.php:205,208` — badge "Extension"
- `templates/order/extension-view.php:75` — title "Quote from seller"
- `templates/order/order-view.php:1514` — pending card title "Quote for extra work"
- `src/Services/NotificationService.php:1099` — notification title "Quote for extra work"
**Issue:** Users see a badge "Extension" in their orders list, click in, and see a title "Quote from seller" with no word "extension" visible. They will wonder if those are two different things.
**Suggested:** Keep "Extension" as the system/badge word; in user-facing body copy, add a parenthetical first time: "Quote from seller (extension to your order)" on extension-view.php. Or rename badge to "Extra work" to match the email heading.

### M10 — "Extra work approved" vendor title vs "Extension paid" email subject vs "Extension approved" email heading
**Files:**
- `templates/order/extension-view.php:65` — UI title: "Extra work approved"
- `src/Services/EmailService.php:741` — email subject: `[%s] Extension paid — continue working`
- `src/Services/EmailService.php:752` — email heading: "Extension paid"
- `src/Services/NotificationService.php:1108` — notification title: "Extra work approved"
**Issue:** Vendor gets three names for the same event (extension cleared payment). Email subject says "Extension paid", email heading says "Extension paid", in-app notification says "Extra work approved", UI page says "Extra work approved". Two clusters, not one, makes it harder to search inbox for a specific transaction.
**Suggested:** Pick one cluster. Recommend "Extra work paid — continue working" across all three surfaces. "Extra work" matches the friendly language on the CTA.

### M11 — Analytics "Tips Received" helper says "net" while the main "Net Earnings" card says "after platform fee"
**File:** `wp-sell-services-pro/templates/dashboard/sections/analytics.php:181,192`
**Current:** Net Earnings card: "Last 30 Days · after platform fee". Tips Received card: "Last 30 Days · net".
**Issue:** Same concept, two shorthands. New users won't equate "net" with "after platform fee" instantly.
**Suggested:** Tips Received helper: "Last 30 Days · after platform fee" to match.

## Patterns / cross-cutting

### P1 — "Phase" vs "Milestone" vs "Milestone-based" used interchangeably
Single concept, three labels. Full map:

| Surface | Word used |
|---|---|
| `single-request.php:523` legend | "Contract type" |
| `single-request.php:530` radio | "Milestone-based" |
| `single-request.php:573,579` modal body | "phase(s)" |
| `single-request.php:302,322,349` breakdown | "milestone plan", "milestone breakdown", "Project total" |
| `order-view.php:1198-1206` section heading | "Milestones (N)" |
| `order-view.php:1213` button | "+ Propose Milestone" |
| `order-view.php:1340` modal title | "Propose a Milestone" |
| `order-view.php:1353` field label | "Phase title" |
| `milestone-view.php:75,87` title | "Milestone approved" |
| `milestone-view.php:104` subtitle | phase_title |
| All 4 milestone emails | "milestone" in subject, "phase" in body |
| `sales.php:159-166` list row | "Milestone: {phase_title}" |

**Recommendation:** The intentional split looks like "Milestone = the container / system noun, Phase = the user-facing unit noun". That works, but it requires discipline. Apply this rule:
- System-level headers, badges, notifications, email subjects → "Milestone"
- Individual item titles, button labels for single items, field labels → "Phase"

Then H1, H2, H3, H4 above fall into place. Currently applied ~60% of the time.

### P2 — Capitalisation drift across button labels
Buttons across the 1.1.0 feature set use two conventions simultaneously. Sentence-case wins roughly 2:1:

Sentence case: "Cancel proposal", "Open the order", "Review & approve", "View original order", "Approve delivery", "Resubmit delivery", "Start work on this phase", "Submit delivery" (list), "View milestone", "View your earnings", "View your sales"

Title Case: "Submit Delivery" (modal), "Review & Approve" (email), "Accept & Pay", "Send Quote to Buyer", "Send to Buyer", "Request Withdrawal", "Cancel", "Decline", "Save Changes", "Upload Photo"

**Recommendation:** Pick sentence case for new 1.1.0 features (matches most of the new buttons). Leave legacy 1.0 buttons ("Upload Photo", "Save Changes") alone to avoid churn.

### P3 — "Wallet" vs "Balance" vs "Earnings" vs "Revenue"
Same pool of money, four names:

| Surface | Label |
|---|---|
| `sales.php:101` vendor stat card | "Revenue" |
| `analytics.php:179,234` | "Net Earnings" / "Revenue Over Time" |
| `analytics.php:190` | "Tips Received" |
| `wallet.php:91` | "Available Balance" |
| `wallet.php:102,107` | "Total Earned" |
| `wallet.php:141,174` | "Earnings Ledger" / "Earned" |
| Email templates | "wallet", "balance", "earnings" used interchangeably |
| In-app notifications | "credited" + no noun |

**Recommendation:** Adopt this split:
- **Earnings** = historical what-you-made (pre-and-post-fee same bucket, with helper text if net)
- **Wallet / Balance** = the withdrawable pot right now
- **Revenue** → retire in favour of "Earnings" on vendor side (keep "revenue" for admin-only surfaces)

The analytics "Revenue Over Time" chart title inherits the old label — it is the only remaining "Revenue" word on the vendor dashboard and should be renamed to "Net earnings over time".

### P4 — "Quote" vs "Extension" vs "Extra work"
Same feature, three nouns:

| Surface | Word |
|---|---|
| Order-view CTA title (vendor) | "Buyer asked for extra work?" |
| Order-view CTA button | "Send Quote to Buyer" |
| Order-view buyer card title | "Quote for extra work" |
| Order-view vendor pending card | "Extension awaiting buyer" |
| Extension-view title (buyer, pending) | "Quote from seller" |
| Extension-view title (buyer, paid) | "Extra work paid" |
| Sales/orders list badge | "Extension" |
| Email subject (proposed) | "[site] Quote for extra work from your seller" |
| Email subject (approved) | "[site] Extension paid — continue working" |
| Email subject (declined) | "[site] Extension declined" |
| Email heading (approved) | "Extension paid" |
| Notification (approved) | "Extra work approved" |
| Notification (rejected) | "Quote declined" |

**Recommendation:** Same two-noun discipline as P1:
- System / badges / subjects → "Extension"
- User-facing body / CTAs / titles → "Extra work" (or "Quote" when referring to the proposal object specifically)

Specifically rename: email subject "Extension paid" → "Extra work paid"; notification title "Extension paid" → "Extra work paid"; UI title "Extension awaiting buyer" → "Extra work awaiting buyer payment".
