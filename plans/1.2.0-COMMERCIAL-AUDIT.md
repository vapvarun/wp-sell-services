# 1.2.0 — Commercial Audit & Go-to-Market Plan

**Status:** Planning companion to `1.2.0-SERVICE-TYPES.md`
**Targets:** Pro 1.2.0 / 1.3.0 / 1.4.0 releases
**Author:** Varun
**Last updated:** 2026-04-14
**Purpose:** Business-side assessment of the polymorphic service types plan — is it worth building, who's the customer, how do we price it, how do we sell it, what are the commercial risks

---

## Executive summary

The 4-type polymorphic service system (standard service + download + booking + rental) transforms WP Sell Services from "another WordPress service marketplace plugin" into **the only WordPress plugin that combines four distinct marketplace verticals under one license**.

**Commercial verdict:** worth building. The combined position is defensible, the individual types each address large markets with weak WordPress competition, and the pricing leverage unlocked by bookings + rentals justifies 2-3x revenue per customer vs current Pro tier.

**Biggest value drivers:** bookings (highest pricing leverage, biggest customer segment) and rentals (biggest competitive moat, highest price ceiling).

**Biggest risk:** timing. Every week slipped is a week a competitor could ship. Release dates must be protected even at cost of trimming scope.

**Bottom-line recommendation:** single Pro SKU at **$299/year** post-1.4.0, up from current tier. Grandfathered discount for existing customers. Four marketing motions targeting four personas, each converting through a different entry point on the same product.

## 2. Combined commercial position

### What the 4-type Pro SKU actually becomes

**Not** "another service marketplace plugin."

**It becomes:** the only WordPress plugin that lets you build a multi-vertical service marketplace combining:

- Service delivery (freelance work)
- Digital product sales (downloads)
- Time-based bookings (coaching, consulting, sessions)
- Space rental (rooms, studios, venues, meeting spaces)

### Each type alone fights a different battle

| Standalone angle | Competition |
|---|---|
| "Services only" | Dokan / WC Vendors |
| "Downloads only" | Easy Digital Downloads |
| "Bookings only" | LatePoint / Amelia |
| "Rentals only" | Motopress Hotel Booking |

### All four together fights nobody directly

No competitor in WordPress offers this combination. The **integration is the moat** — one commission engine, one wallet, one payout flow, one review system across 4 types from a single SKU.

### Competitive landscape at a glance

| Competitor | What they do | What they DON'T do |
|---|---|---|
| Dokan | Multi-vendor services/WooCommerce | Bookings, rentals, native downloads |
| WC Vendors | Multi-vendor WooCommerce | Bookings, rentals, purpose-built downloads |
| LatePoint | Single-vendor bookings | Marketplace, downloads, rentals |
| Amelia | Single-vendor bookings | Marketplace, downloads, rentals |
| Easy Digital Downloads | Single-vendor downloads | Marketplace, bookings, rentals |
| Motopress Hotel Booking | Single-vendor hotel booking | Marketplace, bookings, downloads, services |
| BuddyBoss + addons | Community + some marketplace | Native unified types, bookings, rentals |

**Closest full-combo competitor:** a custom-built marketplace stitching 5 separate plugins together. That's the existing "solution" and it's a terrible developer and user experience.

## 3. Customer personas

Each release unlocks different personas. Pricing and marketing should speak to each.

### Persona 1 — Existing WP Sell Services customer

- **Current state:** Uses the free plugin or Pro for services today
- **1.2.0 unlock:** Can now also sell downloads to the same audience
- **1.3.0 unlock:** Can add booking/consulting sessions
- **1.4.0 unlock:** Can rent their physical space if they have one
- **Sales motion:** email to existing customer list per release, "your Pro license now includes..." — pure upsell, zero acquisition cost
- **Pricing sensitivity:** low — grandfathered + discount

### Persona 2 — Creator / infopreneur

- **Current state:** Selling courses, PDFs, templates on Gumroad or their own WP site with EDD
- **Pain point:** Gumroad takes 10% per sale. EDD is single-vendor — can't build a marketplace of creators
- **What we offer:** "Stop paying Gumroad's fee. Own your marketplace on WordPress with downloads + bookings + direct customer relationships"
- **Unlock release:** 1.2.0 (downloads) + 1.3.0 (add coaching as a separate revenue stream)
- **Pricing sensitivity:** medium — used to paying Gumroad percentage fees, so flat $199-299/year feels cheap
- **Entry point:** Google search "Gumroad alternative WordPress", "digital download marketplace plugin"

### Persona 3 — Coach / consultant / therapist / tutor

- **Current state:** Using Calendly ($8-16/user/month) + WP site + manual Stripe integration
- **Pain point:** Calendly data lives on Calendly's servers. Customer relationships mediated by a third party. Separate tools for scheduling + payments + content
- **What we offer:** "Replace Calendly and your payment gateway with one WordPress plugin. Own your booking data and customer relationships"
- **Unlock release:** 1.3.0 (bookings is the main draw)
- **Pricing sensitivity:** **low** — already paying Calendly + Stripe + WP hosting. $299/year is **cheaper than their current stack**
- **Entry point:** "Calendly alternative WordPress", "self-hosted booking plugin", coaching community recommendations

### Persona 4 — Space operator (studio owner, venue host, co-working operator)

- **Current state:** Listing on Peerspace / Giggster / Splacer, paying 15-30% commission per booking, minimal control over branding or customer data
- **Pain point:** giving up 15-30% of every booking. No brand control. No customer relationship. Peerspace owns the audience
- **What we offer:** "Build your own Peerspace on WordPress. Keep 100% of your revenue. Own your customer data. Control your branding"
- **Unlock release:** 1.4.0 (rentals is the main draw)
- **Pricing sensitivity:** **very low** — currently giving up 15%+ per booking. Paying $299-499/year is trivial compared to saving 15% on every transaction
- **Entry point:** Google search "Peerspace alternative", "self-hosted venue booking", industry forums (photo studio communities, co-working operator Slack groups)

### Persona 5 — Agency building a marketplace for a client

- **Current state:** Quoting $10,000-50,000 custom builds using Dokan + LatePoint + 3 other plugins stitched together
- **Pain point:** integration complexity, ongoing support of 5 plugin dependencies, client delays
- **What we offer:** "One plugin, one license, one vendor to support. Deliver your client's marketplace in days, not months"
- **Unlock release:** progressive — each release unlocks more agency use cases
- **Pricing sensitivity:** **zero** — agencies pass cost to clients. They'll pay whatever the license costs
- **Entry point:** agency Slack communities, WP Tavern coverage, word-of-mouth at agency meetups

### The insight

Personas 3 and 4 (coaches + space operators) are currently paying recurring SaaS fees or commission fees that **dwarf** any reasonable annual license price. Even a $499/year Pro license is a bargain compared to their current spending.

**This is where the real pricing power lives.** The marketing focus should be disproportionately on these two personas because they have the strongest commercial case to buy.

## 4. Pricing strategy

### Current state (pre-1.2.0)

Likely $79-$149/year based on typical WP plugin Pro pricing. (Actual current price should be verified.)

### Post-1.4.0 — recommended single SKU at $299/year

| What's included | Who it's for |
|---|---|
| All 4 service types | Every persona |
| Unlimited vendors | Every persona |
| All gateways (Stripe, PayPal, Razorpay, Offline) | Every persona |
| Commission engine, wallet, payouts | Every persona |
| Analytics, audit log, REST API | Every persona |
| Email templates + admin overrides | Every persona |
| Gutenberg blocks | Every persona |
| Standard support (community + ticket) | Every persona |

**Why single SKU:**

- Easier marketing ("one price, everything included")
- Supports the "no addon culture" decision from Q1
- $299 is below pain threshold for personas 3 and 4 (their alternative costs are 5-10x this)
- Existing customers grandfathered for renewal cycle → auto-upgrade on next renewal
- Net revenue per customer increases 2-3x vs current Pro tier

### Alternative — tiered SKUs

If single SKU doesn't work commercially, fall back to three tiers:

| Tier | Price/year | What's included | Target persona |
|---|---|---|---|
| Starter Pro | $149 | Downloads + Standard services + basic Pro features | Existing customers, creators (Persona 2) |
| Marketplace Pro | $299 | Everything in Starter + Bookings | Coaches, consultants (Persona 3) |
| Enterprise Pro | $599 | Everything in Marketplace + Rentals + white label + priority support | Space operators, agencies (Personas 4, 5) |

**Why single SKU is better:**

- Simpler support ("do I have bookings or not" never a question)
- Simpler upgrade path
- Higher average revenue per customer
- No analysis paralysis at purchase time

**Why tiered might be right:**

- Lower entry point captures price-sensitive customers
- Clearer upgrade path for existing customers
- Better for A/B testing what customers actually value

**Recommendation:** start with single SKU at $299. If conversion is too slow, introduce a $149 Starter tier later as a price ladder entry.

### Grandfathering strategy

- **Existing Pro customers on current license:** keep current price for the duration of their current license term
- **At renewal after 1.2.0 ships:** offered 20% discount if they renew before 1.4.0 ships (creates urgency, captures downloads + bookings value at discount)
- **After 1.4.0 renewal:** full new price applies
- **New customers:** pay new price from day one starting with 1.2.0 launch

Messaging: *"Your existing Pro license continues to work. When you renew, you'll get all the new service types automatically. Lock in your current price by renewing before {1.4.0 launch date} and keep 20% off for the life of your license."*

## 7. Risk assessment

### Commercial risks

**Risk 1 — Scope creep delays all three releases**

11 weeks planned → 20 weeks in reality is plausible. Market windows are real; if LatePoint ships a marketplace feature or Amelia adds multi-vendor while we're still building, our moat shrinks.

**Mitigation:** ruthlessly protect the rollout sequencing. Free 1.2.0 ships first, alone, no marketing. Pro 1.2.0 ships minimal (downloads only). Don't chain releases — each can ship independently when ready. Commit to internal deadlines even if not external.

**Risk 2 — Support burden balloons**

4 types = 4x the failure modes. Bookings specifically have timezone/DST bugs, meeting URL bugs, reminder delivery bugs. One bad booking experience = angry customer = bad review.

**Mitigation:** invest in the CLI test harness (already shipping in 1.1.0), onboarding docs per type, live chat support during first 60 days per release, private alpha with real users before public launch.

**Risk 3 — Positioning confusion**

"Multi-vertical marketplace platform" is technically correct but fuzzy. Customers come looking for "the coaching marketplace plugin" or "the rental marketplace plugin" — they don't care about the other three verticals.

**Mitigation:** create type-specific landing pages and ad campaigns. "WP Sell Services Pro for Coaches" / "for Studio Operators" / "for Digital Creators" / "for Freelancers" — same product, different entry points.

**Risk 4 — Existing customers don't upgrade**

If current customers only use standard services, they might not see the value of the new types. Especially if they're on a perpetual license or heavily discounted renewal.

**Mitigation:** email sequences showing customers how to use new types with their existing audience. Free educational content on "adding new revenue streams to your existing marketplace".

**Risk 5 — The WordPress ecosystem weakens**

WordPress core is stagnating, block themes are slow, plugin market consolidating. Customers might increasingly choose SaaS platforms (Shopify, Webflow, Circle, Whop, Kajabi) over self-hosted.

**Mitigation:** lean into the "own your data, own your audience" angle. Self-hosted is a feature, not a bug, for personas 3 and 4. Hedge: the polymorphic type system is portable — could ship as a standalone SaaS later if WP ecosystem softens.

**Risk 6 — Competitors ship similar features**

LatePoint adds marketplace. Amelia adds multi-vendor. Dokan adds booking. Any of these would close the moat.

**Mitigation:** ship first and fast. Establish the category before competitors notice. Marketing language that pre-claims the category: "the first and only", "the original", "the complete" — makes copycats feel like copycats.

### Technical risks (commercial impact)

**Risk 7 — Action Scheduler reliability on cheap shared hosts**

Real-cron requirement excludes low-end hosting. Customers on basic shared hosts (Bluehost, GoDaddy) might have a terrible booking experience.

**Mitigation:** warning banner + documentation + list of recommended hosts. Consider refusing to enable booking/rental features on sites without detected real cron.

**Risk 8 — Timezone bugs burn customer trust**

One wrong booking time email and a customer misses their session. They blame us, not their timezone setting.

**Mitigation:** extensive CLI test coverage + manual QA across 10+ timezones + real-user alpha testing before public launch. Also: email the vendor if the buyer misses a session — creates an escalation channel.

### Opportunity cost — what we're NOT doing

With the same 11 weeks we could do instead:

- Major frontend redesign (admin UX improvements)
- Mobile app (vendor + buyer apps)
- Integrations with existing tools (Zapier, Make, Webhooks, Slack notifications)
- Performance work on existing standard services flow
- Multilingual / i18n improvements
- Accessibility improvements

**Counterpoint:** those are all good ideas but none of them **change the product's position in the market** the way adding 3 new types does. Those are incremental improvements; the type system is a platform shift.

**But if 11 weeks of uninterrupted engineering isn't realistic:** the minimum viable commercial expansion is Free 1.2.0 foundation + Pro 1.3.0 bookings. Skip downloads and rentals. That's 6 weeks of work and still unlocks the highest-leverage persona.

## 8. Recommended adjustments to the plan

Three adjustments based on the commercial audit:

### Adjustment 1 — Keep current release order (downloads first)

The technical plan ships downloads in 1.2.0, bookings in 1.3.0, rentals in 1.4.0. Commercially, bookings has higher leverage — but the engineering simplicity of downloads as the first consumer of the new architecture outweighs the commercial argument.

**Keep the current order.** Downloads is the architecturally smallest new type, so shipping it first de-risks the bigger releases. Shipping a complete product flow in 2 weeks is a morale and trust win.

### Adjustment 2 — Build a polished demo site before 1.2.0 ships

Before Pro 1.2.0 launches, build a realistic demo at `demo.wpsellservices.com` showing a template marketplace selling Lightroom presets, WordPress themes, stock photos, and PDFs. Free sample downloads drive SEO traffic. The demo becomes the case study.

**Why:** most WordPress plugin websites have terrible demos or none. A polished demo that looks like a real marketplace is a conversion tool AND a credibility builder. Worth ~3 days to set up and ~2 days to populate.

**Cost:** ~1 week of content/design work per release, not in the engineering plan. Marketing responsibility, not engineering.

### Adjustment 3 — Private alpha with real users for 1.3.0 and 1.4.0

Before public launch of Pro 1.3.0 and Pro 1.4.0, run a private alpha with 10-20 real users from personas 3 and 4. Bookings and rentals have failure modes that don't show up in CLI tests or manual QA. Real users with real timezones, real calendars, real customers, real edge cases will find bugs we can't anticipate.

**Cost:** ~2 weeks of alpha per release, overlapping with the final engineering sprint. Participants get free lifetime licenses in exchange for weekly feedback calls.

**Benefit:** the first 20 public customers are vastly better than the alpha 20 because we've already fixed their problems. Zero public refund requests in the first month. Testimonials and case studies come from the alpha cohort.

## 10. Bottom line

**Is this plan commercially beneficial?**

Yes. The combined 4-type position is defensible, each individual type addresses a large market with weak WordPress competition, and the pricing leverage from bookings and rentals justifies 2-3x revenue per customer.

**If you could only build ONE new type:** bookings. Highest leverage, biggest persona, highest pricing power.

**If you only have 6 weeks:** ship Free 1.2.0 + Pro 1.3.0 bookings. Skip downloads and rentals. Still the minimum viable commercial expansion.

**If you have the full 11 weeks:** ship all three. The combined moat is worth more than the sum of its parts.

**How do we sell it?**

One SKU, one price ($299/year post-1.4.0), four marketing motions:

1. **Email existing customers** → upsell path, free marketing, highest-margin revenue
2. **Creator persona** (downloads) → SEO, content, Gumroad-alternative positioning
3. **Coach persona** (bookings) → Calendly-alternative positioning, influencer seeding, WP community coverage
4. **Space operator persona** (rentals) → direct outreach, comparison content, partnerships with themes and agencies

**Biggest risk is timing.** Every week slipped is a week a competitor could ship. Release dates must be protected even at the cost of trimming scope.

**Biggest opportunity is the combined moat.** No WordPress plugin offers this mix. First-mover positioning in a category nobody currently occupies. Defensible for years if executed on schedule.

**Action items separate from the technical plan:**

1. Verify current Pro pricing (need actual number before finalizing the $299 recommendation)
2. Build demo site infrastructure (~1 week, marketing/design work)
3. Recruit alpha cohorts for 1.3.0 and 1.4.0 (~20 users each, start recruiting 4 weeks before each launch)
4. Draft the 4 persona landing pages (can start now, iterate as releases ship)
5. Update homepage messaging for "Services + Downloads" by 1.2.0 launch
6. Coordinate press outreach 2 weeks before each release

**Commercial planning is complete.** Technical execution starts when given the go-ahead on the `1.2.0-SERVICE-TYPES.md` plan.
